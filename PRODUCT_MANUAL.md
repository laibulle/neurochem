# SUBMISSYN Product Manual - Physical/Digital Bridge

## Concept

The **product manual** is a chilling artifact designed to bridge the physical and digital worlds of your dark romance lore. It's meant to be discovered by the "victim" - creating a moment of horror, curiosity, and inevitable descent into the narrative.

## The Scenario

Imagine this scene:
1. The victim finds an empty vial or bottle
2. There's a QR code or URL on the label
3. They scan it out of curiosity, fear, or confusion
4. They land on the manual page
5. They realize what they've been given
6. The rabbit hole beckons...

## What Makes It Effective

### 1. **Dual Audience**
The manual is written **for the aggressor** (the buyer/administrator) but **discovered by the victim**. This creates psychological tension:
- Clinical, cold language as if they're not even human
- Referred to as "subject" throughout
- Makes it more disturbing than if it were written for them

### 2. **Plausible Deniability**
- "Research purposes only"
- "Not approved for human use"
- Legal disclaimers everywhere
- Makes it feel MORE real because of the CYA language

### 3. **Mystery Over Explicitness**
- Hints at effects without graphic detail
- "Observable indicators" rather than "what will happen to you"
- Leaves room for imagination (which is scarier)
- They'll search for more information → deeper into lore

### 4. **The Hook at the Bottom**
```
"If you are reading this and did not purchase this product yourself:
You may have questions. You deserve answers."
```
- Acknowledges they might be the victim
- Validates their fear and confusion
- Invites them to explore further
- Creates curiosity despite terror

## Writing Style Notes

### What Works
- ✅ Cold, clinical language
- ✅ Passive voice ("observable indicators")
- ✅ Medical/pharmaceutical jargon
- ✅ Bureaucratic disclaimer language
- ✅ Treating humans like research subjects

### What to Avoid
- ❌ Overly explicit sexual content
- ❌ Graphic violence
- ❌ Too much detail (mystery is key)
- ❌ Cartoon villain language
- ❌ Breaking the "research use" facade

## Printing Tips

### For Physical Props
1. **Browser print** works well - page is styled for printing
2. **Recommended paper:** Slightly yellowed or aged-looking
3. **Fold style:** Medical insert tri-fold or booklet
4. **Optional:** Coffee stain for "found document" aesthetic
5. **Size:** Fits in small vial box or rolled in larger bottle

### Print Settings
- Black and white or color (red accents effective in color)
- Remove headers/footers in print dialog
- Scale to fit page
- Portrait orientation
